# Iron Throne of Ashes — Player's Manual

*A digital board game for 2–4 players · Session length: 60–90 minutes*

---

## The World Is Burning

For a thousand years, the Four Elemental Courts held an uneasy peace. The Heartstone — an artifact of world-bending power housed in the Hall of Neutrality — prevented any one dynasty from claiming dominance. As long as it sat in neutral ground, no Court could move against the others without tearing the Compact apart.

Then the Shadowking rose from the Blighted Wastes.

He did not negotiate. He did not declare war. He simply walked to the Hall of Neutrality, took the Heartstone, and retreated to his Dark Fortress. The Compact shattered the moment his hand closed around it.

Now the Doom Toll — a great spectral bell — rings out across the Known Lands each time the Shadowking's grip tightens. It has rung seven times. When it strikes thirteen, the Blighted Wastes will consume everything.

You are an Arch-Regent of one of the Four Elemental Courts. Your Fellowship stands ready. The Known Lands stretch between you and the Dark Fortress. And three other Arch-Regents — rivals, allies, and possibly a traitor — march on the same land.

This is not a game about defeating the Shadowking. It is a game about **who bears the cost of trying.**

---

## The Four Elemental Courts

You will play as one of four Arch-Regents, each commanding a Court with a distinct history and homeland.

**The Court of the Deep Root** rules from ancient stone fortresses buried in primeval forest. Their armies march in siege-weight armor; their Artificers know the patience of mountains.

**The Court of the Gale Throne** holds spire citadels above the clouds. They move fast and light, their Heralds riding messenger birds between strongholds.

**The Court of the Ember Crown** built their power in forge cities along volcanic borderlands. Their Artificers are the finest in the Known Lands — and they know it.

**The Court of the Tide Seal** commands coastal strongholds and river networks. Their supply lines span the Known Lands; their Heralds have walked every road.

All four Courts begin on equal footing. Your Court's identity is a matter of aesthetics and pride, not mechanical advantage. The first Arch-Regent to master the board — not their birthright — will hold the Heartstone when the smoke clears.

---

## What You're Playing For

There are three ways this story ends:

**Territory Victory.** The Heartstone sits at the Dark Fortress, guarded by Death Knights. Reclaim it — and hold the most Strongholds at the end of any round — and you win. A dynasty without land is just a title. You need both.

**The Shadowking Wins.** If the Doom Toll strikes thirteen before any Arch-Regent can reclaim the Heartstone, the Blighted Wastes swallow the Known Lands. All Arch-Regents lose. (In Blood Pact mode, the traitor in your midst wins alone.)

**A Draw — All Courts Broken.** If every Arch-Regent simultaneously falls into Broken Court state — reduced, humbled, unable to act fully — the game ends without a victor. A dynasty without strength is still a dynasty. But none of you go home proud.

The board will always show you where every player stands: how many Strongholds each Court controls, how many War Banners they hold, whether any player has fallen to Broken Court status. You will never have to guess whether you're winning. The question is always what you're going to do about it.

## What You Need to Know Before You Start

### The Board: The Known Lands

The Known Lands are a network of 28 Strongholds connected by roads and paths. You move your Fellowship from node to node along these connections — you cannot jump across the map. Every Stronghold you control is a base of power. Every Stronghold your enemies control is a staging ground against you.

**Standard Strongholds** (22 nodes) are the territory you'll fight over. Claim them by moving onto them and spending a War Banner.

**Forge Keep Strongholds** (4 nodes) are the great prize of the Known Lands. A Court whose Artificer occupies a Forge Keep generates **three** War Banners per turn instead of one. Control a Forge Keep and your engine hums. Control two and every other player starts watching you very carefully.

**The Dark Fortress** is the Shadowking's seat of power. It cannot be claimed. Death Knights and Blight Wraiths move outward from here. A Herald brave enough to walk there alone — and find it unguarded — can attempt a Diplomatic Action, reducing the Doom Toll without a fight.

**The Hall of Neutrality** is where the Heartstone once rested. It remains neutral ground throughout the game — but the Heartstone itself is now at the Dark Fortress, and that is where you must go to reclaim it.

### War Banners: The Currency of Power

War Banners are the single resource that drives everything. You spend them to move (1 Banner per Stronghold you pass through), claim territory (1 Banner), and add strength to your combat rolls. Your Artificers generate them. Forge Keeps multiply them.

War Banners do not carry over between rounds. At the end of each round, unspent Banners are lost. Economy is motion — if you're not spending, you're falling behind.

### Fate Cards: Your Personal Hand

Fate Cards serve two roles in the game — and it's important to understand which is which.

**Personal Hand.** You maintain a hand of Fate Cards that persists between rounds. These cards pay for Voting Phase COUNTERs and Rescue donations. You start with 3 cards. At the end of each Production Phase, you draw back up to your hand limit.

**Combat Draws.** When you enter War Field combat, you draw Fate Cards directly from the shared deck for that fight only. These cards are temporary — they are discarded after combat resolves and never enter or leave your personal hand.

**Hand limit.** Your base hand limit is 3. Each Herald beyond the first in your Fellowship raises this limit by 1, up to a maximum of 6.

| Heralds in Fellowship | Hand Limit |
|----------------------|------------|
| 0–1 | 3 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 5 |
| 4+ | 6 |

**Surplus.** If you lose a Herald and your hand now exceeds your limit, you keep the surplus until you spend it. No forced discard.

### The Shared Deck

All Fate Cards — combat draws, personal hands, Voting Phase counters — are drawn from a single shared deck. When that deck runs dry and reshuffles, the Doom Toll advances by 1 before the first new card is drawn. The deck's remaining count is always visible on the board. A shallow deck is a ticking clock. A table that forgets to watch it will be surprised at the worst moment.

### Your Fellowship

Each Court begins with the same four characters. Their Power Levels combine with your War Banners to determine your strength in combat.

| Character | Power Level | What They Do |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Arch-Regent** | 8 | Your leader. Always with you — can never be lost. Determines how many Fate Cards you draw in combat. |
| **Knight** | 6 | Your warband's fighting core. Claims Strongholds, challenges Death Knights, wins battles. Cannot recruit. |
| **Herald** | 0 | Reveals hidden characters across the Known Lands. Alone in hostile territory, walks under Diplomatic Protection. Each additional Herald beyond the first raises your Fate Card hand limit by 1 (max 6). |
| **Artificer** | 3 | Generates War Banners. Their value triples at a Forge Keep. The quiet backbone of your campaign. |

You begin with these four. Over the course of the game, your Herald can recruit **Unknown Wanderers** — 20 hidden characters scattered face-down across the Strongholds. Revealed characters join your Fellowship immediately: Knights to fight, Heralds to explore, Artificers to produce. Your Fellowship can grow to eight characters total.

A Fellowship of only Knights will win every fight. It will also grind to a halt the moment it runs short of Banners, and short Banners mean short options.

## Game Setup

Before the first round, the game prepares the board as follows. If you are playing with the built-in tutorial (*Into the Blighted Wastes*), setup is handled automatically and you begin Turn 1 with guidance. This section describes what a full game setup looks like.

1. **Determine turn order.** The game performs a random draw and displays the fixed Action Phase order for this session — for example, "Court of the Deep Root → Court of the Ember Crown → Court of the Gale Throne → Court of the Tide Seal." This order does not rotate. It is shown in the turn tracker at the top of the screen throughout the game.

2. **Place your Fellowship.** Each Arch-Regent begins at their Court's starting Keep — a Stronghold pre-claimed in your name. No other Court can claim your starting Keep in the first round.

3. **Scatter the Unknown Wanderers.** Twenty character tokens are placed face-down on Standard Strongholds across the Known Lands. You can see where they are; you cannot see what they are until a Herald stands beside them and reveals them.

4. **Set the Doom Toll to zero.** The Toll tracker begins at rest. The bell has not yet rung this game.

   *3-Player:* The Doom Toll begins at position 2, not 0. Three Courts instead of four creates a thinner voting coalition from the first round — one abstention is 33% defection, not 25%. The early toll reflects that structural pressure.

5. **Place the Shadowking's forces.** Two Death Knights emerge from the Dark Fortress, positioned at its edges. No Blight Wraiths are on the board yet — they will come.

6. **Place the Heartstone token at the Dark Fortress.** The Shadowking carried it there when the Compact shattered. The Hall of Neutrality marks where it once rested — a historical note, not its current location.

7. **Prepare the Behavior Deck.** The Shadowking's 20-card deck is shuffled: six SPAWN cards, six MOVE cards, four CLAIM cards, three ASSAULT cards, and one ESCALATE card. This deck drives every Shadowking action for the entire game. When it runs out and reshuffles, the Doom Toll advances by one.

8. **Distribute starting Fate Cards.** Each player draws 3 Fate Cards from the shared deck — their personal hand, used in voting and rescue donations. These are separate from the temporary draws used in combat.

9. **Select your play mode** (if not already chosen in lobby):
   - **Competitive** — Standard play. One player wins by territory when the Heartstone changes hands.
   - **Blood Pact** — One Arch-Regent is secretly dealt the Blood Pact card through the app. Their hidden win condition: let the Doom Toll reach thirteen.
   - **Cooperative** — All players win together or lose together. No PvP combat. Harder Shadowking deck.

## How a Round Works

---

### Phase 1: The Shadowking Acts

The Shadowking draws the top card of his Behavior Deck and the game announces what he intends to do. The card is revealed to all players before the vote — no surprises about what you're being asked to stop.

There are five types of Behavior Cards:

**SPAWN** — Two new Blight Wraiths emerge near the Dark Fortress, beginning their slow spread across the Known Lands. If the Fortress is surrounded, they appear at the farthest reachable node from the leading Arch-Regent's home Keep.

**MOVE** — The Death Knight nearest the leading Arch-Regent (the player with the most Strongholds) advances two nodes toward them. The Shadowking always hunts the strong.

**CLAIM** — The Death Knight farthest from any Fellowship claims the nearest unoccupied Stronghold. The Shadowking builds an empire of his own, one node at a time.

**ASSAULT** — The Death Knight adjacent to the weakest Arch-Regent (lowest current War Banners) initiates War Field combat against them. The Shadowking kicks the people who are already down.

**ESCALATE** — The Doom Toll advances by two. There is no avoiding this one entirely — but you can reduce it to one advance with a unanimous vote.

*In Final Phase (Doom Toll position 10 or higher), two Behavior Cards are drawn and resolved each round.*

---

### Phase 2: The Voting Phase

Every active Arch-Regent now votes simultaneously: **COUNTER** or **ABSTAIN**.

To vote COUNTER, you must spend Fate Cards from your personal hand — 1 card under normal conditions, 2 cards once the Doom Toll reaches Final Phase (position 10 or higher). Your vote choice is hidden until all players have committed. The reveal is simultaneous.

**If every active Arch-Regent votes COUNTER and can afford the Fate Card cost:** the Behavior Card is blocked or reduced. (SPAWN wraiths appear but cannot move. MOVE and CLAIM actions are cancelled. ASSAULT is cancelled. ESCALATE becomes +1 instead of +2.)

**If any Arch-Regent abstains — or cannot afford the cards:** the Behavior Card resolves at full effect, and the Doom Toll advances by one.

A player in Broken Court state votes at full standing. Being reduced does not silence you at the table. This is not optional — it is written into the structure of the game. Broken Courts still vote.

A player with zero Fate Cards cannot COUNTER — they have nothing to spend. The game will flag this for you before the vote timer expires.

The political weight of the Voting Phase lies here: the Arch-Regent with the most Strongholds has the most to lose if the Doom Toll advances. The Arch-Regents trailing behind have the least. Everyone at the table knows this math. Cooperation is always expensive. Refusing to cooperate is always dangerous. Someone blinks first every round.

*3-Player:* With three voters, unanimity requires everyone. There is no swing vote and no majority to hide behind. Each abstention carries more weight than in a four-player game. Expect more Doom Toll advancement per session; budget your Fate Cards accordingly.

---

### Phase 3: Player Action Phases

After the vote resolves, each Arch-Regent takes their turn in the fixed order established at setup. On your turn, you have **two actions** (or **one action** if you are in Broken Court state).

You may spend your actions on any combination of the following:

**Move your Fellowship.** Pay 1 War Banner per Stronghold node you cross. You may move your entire Fellowship together or split characters to different nodes — but splitting means weaker pieces moving alone, which has its own risks and uses.

**Claim a Stronghold.** If your Fellowship occupies an unclaimed Stronghold, spend 1 War Banner to plant your banner there. It now contributes to your territory count.

**Recruit.** If a Herald in your Fellowship is adjacent to a face-down Unknown Wanderer token, spend 1 action to reveal it. The character joins your Fellowship immediately. A Fellowship without a Herald cannot recruit.

**Initiate War Field combat.** If your Fellowship shares a node with an enemy Fellowship, combat resolves. This is not optional once Fellowships occupy the same node at the end of a movement action — if you move into contested territory, the War Field triggers. (See *Combat: The War Field* below.)

**Rescue a Broken Court.** If an allied Arch-Regent is in Broken Court state, you may spend one of your actions to donate 2–5 Fate Cards to them. They restore War Banners equal to the cards donated, clear all Penalty Cards, and return to full active status immediately. You spend the action; they recover. The political value of a rescue extends beyond the mechanical one — a rescued Arch-Regent knows whose vote they owe.

**Reclaim the Heartstone.** If your Fellowship occupies the Dark Fortress and no Death Knights are present there, spend 1 action to perform the Reclaim action. The Heartstone token moves to your Court's HUD — visible to all players at all times. No War Banner cost. The Reclaim option is greyed out, not hidden, when the Fortress is guarded.

If you hold the Heartstone and lose a War Field combat (against any opponent), the Heartstone drops to your current node as a free token. Any Fellowship occupying that node may claim it with 1 action.

**Herald Diplomatic Action.** If your Herald has traveled to the Dark Fortress alone — no other Fellowship characters accompanying them — and the Fortress is unoccupied by Death Knights or Blight Wraiths, spend one action to perform a Diplomatic Action. The Doom Toll recedes by two. A Herald who completes this action cannot perform it again in the same game; their one diplomatic mission is spent. This is not combat. This is negotiation.

*The Diplomatic Action is not an early exploration move. It is a once-per-game sacrifice. The Doom Toll reduction is significant — two positions from the edge can mean two more rounds of survival. But it costs your Herald's movement and recruitment potential for the rest of the game, and it requires dangerous, solitary travel to the Shadowking's seat of power. Use it when the Toll is at 9 or above and there is no better option. Use it because you have no other choice.*

---

### Phase 4: Production and Round End

After all players have taken their action phases, Artificers generate War Banners for their controlling Court.

- Each Artificer generates **1 War Banner** per turn.
- An Artificer occupying a Forge Keep Stronghold generates **3 War Banners** per turn.

War Banner totals are always open information — every player can see how many Banners each Court holds.

At the end of the round, all unspent War Banners are discarded. They do not carry forward. Use them or lose them.

Each player also draws Fate Cards from the shared deck back up to their hand limit. Cards already in your hand are kept — only the deficit is drawn.

## The Politics of the Known Lands

This game is a negotiation about who pays for collective survival. Every Voting Phase is a moment where each Arch-Regent asks: do I spend to contain the Shadowking, or do I let the Toll advance and put pressure on the player who benefits most from stability?

That tension is not a flaw. It is the game's engine.

**The check-the-leader dynamic.** The player leading in Strongholds — or controlling two Forge Keeps, or holding the Heartstone — has the most to lose if the Doom Toll advances. The players behind have less to lose. This asymmetry is intentional: the leader is structurally pressured to spend more Fate Cards than anyone else. Abstaining is a legal form of political taxation on the frontrunner.

When three players abstain on a SPAWN card that was going to pressure the leader anyway, they are not sabotaging the game. They are applying pressure that the leader must buy off or absorb. That is how the Voting Phase works.

**When to let the Toll tick.** There are rounds where the correct play is to let the Doom Toll advance by one rather than protect the winning player. A Toll at 6, a clear leader at 8 Strongholds: every trailing player has reason to hold their cards close. The leader should feel the weight of their position. They will either spend to stop it or start falling behind — which is the intended outcome.

**When to hold the line.** There are also rounds where unanimous COUNTER is correct regardless of standings. An ASSAULT card hitting the weakest player when Doom is at 9 is not a political opportunity; it is a crisis. A table that disagrees about when to hold and when to let slip is a table playing the game exactly right.

**Forge Keep pressure.** A Court controlling two Forge Keeps generates roughly four times the War Banners of an unfortified rival. The designed response is not to match this mechanically — it is to vote against that player's interests until they are forced to fight for every Stronghold they hold. The social pressure of the Voting Phase is the check on Forge Keep dominance. Let the Toll tick when the MOVE card targets them. Abstain when they are the only one who needs the COUNTER. This is the intended dynamic, not a house rule.

**The strongest player should feel the weight of their lead.** That weight is the Voting Phase. If you are winning and the table is not cooperating, that is not betrayal — it is the game working as designed.

## Combat: The War Field

When two Fellowships occupy the same Stronghold node at the end of a movement action, a War Field resolves. Combat is not a choice at this point — if you move your Fellowship into occupied territory, the War Field happens.

A player in Broken Court state **cannot initiate** a War Field — but they **can defend** if an enemy Fellowship moves into their node.

**Resolving the War Field:**

1. **Calculate Base Strength** for each side: the sum of all character Power Levels in the Fellowship, plus the current War Banner count.

2. **The Attacker draws Fate Cards.** The number of cards drawn equals the ceiling of their Arch-Regent's Power Level divided by four. With a standard Arch-Regent (Power Level 8), that's 2 cards. The attacker selects one and plays it **face-down.**

3. **The Defender draws one fewer Fate Card** (minimum 1), selects one, and plays it **face-up.** The Defender didn't choose this fight — seeing their card in the open is a structural compensation.

4. **Both cards are revealed simultaneously.** Each side adds their card's value to their Base Strength. (Card values range from −1 to +4, with many cards at 0. Negative values reduce strength; the floor is 0.)

5. **Higher total wins. Ties go to the Defender.**

6. **The loser draws Penalty Cards** equal to the margin — the difference in final totals.

**Example:** You have 3 Knights (6+6+6 = 18 Power) and 5 War Banners. Base strength: 23. Your opponent has 2 Knights (12 Power) and 3 Banners. Base strength: 15. You draw 2 Fate Cards, select one, play it face-down. They draw 1 and play it face-up. Maximum swing in either direction is 5. Your 8-point lead is safe.

If both bases were 15 and 16, those Fate Cards would be the entire fight.

*Fate Cards decide close fights. Team composition dominates.*

**Penalty Cards** accumulate and persist between rounds. They are not discarded at round end. When a player's total Penalty Cards meet or exceed their current War Banner count, that Arch-Regent enters **Broken Court state.**

When you fight a Shadowking force, the rules are the same except Shadowking forces do not draw Fate Cards — their strength is fixed. Death Knights have a base strength of 10. Blight Wraiths have a base strength of 6.

Defeating a Death Knight in War Field combat reduces the Doom Toll by one.

*Note on combat draws:* The Fate Cards drawn here come directly from the shared deck, not from your personal hand. They are discarded after combat. Your hand size is unaffected by combat.

---

> **Watch your Penalty Cards.** The board shows when you — and your rivals — are getting close. At 50% of your War Banner count, your Court slot turns yellow. At 75%, it turns red. Both warnings are visible to every player at the table.

## Broken Court State

When an Arch-Regent's Penalty Cards meet or exceed their War Banner count, they enter Broken Court state. This is not elimination. This is not being sent to the sidelines.

A Broken Court Arch-Regent:
- Is **reduced to 1 action per turn** (down from 2)
- **Cannot initiate** War Field combat
- **Cannot claim** new Strongholds
- **Cannot perform** the Recruit action
- **Retains all existing Stronghold claims** — you do not lose your territory on entry
- **Still votes in every Voting Phase** at full standing — no UI demotion, no reduced weight

You are still in the game. You are still politically relevant. You can still defend your territory, vote to contain the Shadowking, and wait for rescue.

Entering Broken Court state also advances the Doom Toll by one.

**Recovery:** Any active Arch-Regent can spend one action to rescue you. They donate 2–5 Fate Cards from their hand. You restore War Banners equal to the cards received, your Penalty Cards are cleared, and you return to full active status on the spot.

A rescue is not charity. It is an alliance. The rescued player's next vote is politically implied — and everyone at the table knows it.

**The Politics of Rescue**

Rescue looks simple: spend an action, donate cards, your ally recovers. But a rescue is a political act with consequences that outlast the turn.

Rescuing the player who is behind gives you a reliable ally and costs the leader a voter they no longer dominate. Rescuing the player who is ahead shores up the frontrunner — and that frontrunner now holds a vote debt to you. Both can be correct. Neither is obviously correct. The Arch-Regent you rescue knows what they owe you. The whole table knows it too.

There is no objectively right rescue. There are only rescues that serve your position and rescues that don't.

*3-Player:* A Broken Court player is one-third of your voting coalition. Keeping your alliance intact matters more here than in a four-player game. A single player in Broken Court meaningfully reduces your collective capacity to hold the Doom Toll.

## The Doom Toll

The Doom Toll is a 13-position track visible to all players at all times. It is the timer and the stakes floor of the game. Watch it the way you would watch a fire creeping toward a door.

**The Toll advances (+1) when:**
- Any Arch-Regent vote is not unanimous (any abstain, any insufficient Fate Cards)
- The Fate Card deck reshuffles
- A Blight Wraith successfully claims a Forge Keep Stronghold
- Any Arch-Regent enters Broken Court state

**The Toll recedes (−1) when:**
- A Fellowship defeats a Death Knight in War Field combat
- A player reclaims a Forge Keep from Blight Wraith occupation
- Three or more active Arch-Regents vote unanimously *and* all spend the required Fate Cards in a single Voting Phase

**At position 10: Final Phase begins.** The board changes. Two Behavior Cards are drawn and resolved each round instead of one. Blight Wraiths auto-spread to an adjacent unoccupied Stronghold every round regardless of the Behavior Card. The Voting Phase cost increases to two Fate Cards per player. The candles on the UI have been dimming. Now the board's ambient light shifts cold, the Shadowking's silhouette fills the horizon, and every action carries heavier weight.

Your HUD now shows an estimated rounds remaining — a non-binding countdown at minimum doom advance rate. At Toll 10, that reads ~2 rounds. It is not a guarantee. It is a clock.

**When Final Phase Begins**

Final Phase is not more of the same game — it is the second and final opening. The tempo has changed. You have, at most, 2–4 rounds before the Doom Toll resolves one way or another.

**Consolidate or push — there is no third option.** If you are in contention for the Heartstone, abandon everything else and move toward the Dark Fortress now. If you are not in contention, you have two jobs: deny the player who is close, and vote COUNTER in every Voting Phase regardless of the cost. A Fate Card spent on a COUNTER vote is worth more than a Stronghold you claim but cannot defend.

**Territory becomes less valuable.** A Stronghold claimed in the last two rounds is unlikely to matter. A Fate Card conserved through the last two rounds might.

**If you are in Broken Court when Final Phase begins:** Recovery requires a genuine political sacrifice from your rescuer. Do not count on it. You will vote. You will hold your territory. You will wait for a moment that may not come.

The game remembers every decision from here forward. Whoever wins or loses, the final rounds are the story your table will tell about this session.

Resolution, one way or another, arrives within a few rounds of Final Phase. If you reach position 10 and the board is still contested, the ending is imminent.

**At position 13:** The Blighted Wastes arrive. All players lose. The game ends.

## Diplomatic Protection

A Herald who moves to a Stronghold **alone** — no other Fellowship characters on the same node — operates under Diplomatic Protection. While protected, other Arch-Regents cannot initiate a War Field against them. No player can send their Fellowship into that Stronghold to pick a fight with a lone Herald.

Blight Wraiths do not respect Diplomatic Protection. They will occupy a Herald's node regardless.

Protection ends the moment any other Fellowship character joins the Herald's node — including your own. Send the Herald alone if you want the protection. Split your Fellowship deliberately.

This rule rewards the use of Heralds as advance scouts and diplomats. A Herald can walk dangerous roads that a full Fellowship would have to fight through.

---

## The Shadowking's Forces

**Death Knights** (2 at game start, maximum 4) are mobile lieutenants. They pursue the leading Arch-Regent, claim Strongholds, and initiate combat. Their base strength is 10 — defeating one is a significant accomplishment. Killing a Death Knight reduces the Doom Toll by one and removes a persistent threat from the board.

Death Knights cannot enter a Stronghold occupied by a solo Herald on a MOVE card. ASSAULT is a different matter — the Shadowking does not honor Diplomatic Protection when he attacks.

**Blight Wraiths** (0 at start, maximum 9) are static occupation forces. They spread from the Dark Fortress and claim Strongholds they reach, but they do not move again after placement unless a SPAWN card brings new Wraiths. Their base strength is 6. A Blight Wraith claiming a Forge Keep is a minor catastrophe: the Doom Toll advances and your best production node falls under shadow.

## Blood Pact Mode: The Traitor

*Optional. Can be selected at the lobby before game start.*

In Blood Pact mode, one Arch-Regent is secretly dealt the Blood Pact card through the app — invisible to all other players, with no physical shuffle tell. That player has sworn fealty to the Shadowking. Their hidden win condition is the inverse of everyone else's: **let the Doom Toll reach thirteen.**

The Blood Pact Arch-Regent plays normally. They have all the same actions, all the same options. No mechanical rule prevents them from voting to COUNTER, from rescuing allies, from claiming Strongholds. The deception is entirely their own work.

The art of the Blood Pact is subtlety. Abstain too often and the table turns on you. Be too helpful and the Shadowking loses. The Blood Pact player has to lose just enough, vote wrong at just the right moments, and maintain a plausible story.

**Accusation — If the accusation is correct:**
The accusation can be initiated unanimously by all other active Arch-Regents. Each accusing player spends 2 Fate Cards. If the target is the Blood Pact holder:

- Full-screen reveal moment — the same weight as an end-of-game reveal
- The traitor's win condition converts to Territory Victory (the same as everyone else's)
- The traitor loses 3 Fate Cards (minimum 0)
- Doom Toll recedes by 1
- Each accusing player receives 1 Fate Card back (net cost: 1 card each)

**If the accusation is wrong:**
- The accusation is rejected; each accusing player receives 1 Fate Card back (net cost: 1 each — the same as a correct accusation)
- The wrongly accused player gains 1 Fate Card (political vindication)
- That player cannot be accused again for 1 round

**Accusation Cooldown:** After any accusation attempt (success or failure), no new accusation can be initiated for 2 rounds.

**Reading the Table**

The Suspicion Log — accessible when initiating an accusation — shows each player's voting record for the last five rounds: when they abstained, when they Counter-voted, when they were the only one who let the Toll advance. It is not proof. It is evidence.

A traitor who votes COUNTER consistently is doing their job well. A traitor who abstained at Doom Toll 9, alone, has a question to answer.

**2-Player Note:** In a 2-player game, accusation is not available. Blood Pact identity is revealed only at game end.

**3-Player Note:** Accusation requires both remaining players to agree unanimously. With only one other player to convince, the decision is more personal and the stakes are higher. The Suspicion Log becomes more important here — with three voters, patterns in the vote record emerge faster, and the traitor has fewer rounds to maintain cover behind ambiguous plays.

*"When the accusation lands, the game stops. The truth is known. The traitor remains — but they are changed."*

At game end, the Blood Pact identity is revealed to all players regardless. If the Shadowking wins, the traitor's action log is shown to the table. Every abstention. Every delayed rescue. Every vote that cost everyone else.

## Cooperative Mode

*Optional. Can be selected at the lobby before game start.*

In Cooperative mode, all Arch-Regents are fully allied against a harder Shadowking. No War Field combat between player Fellowships is possible — Fellowships may share nodes freely. The win condition is collective: reclaim the Heartstone from the Dark Fortress before the Doom Toll completes.

The Behavior Deck is modified to be more aggressive. Expect more ESCALATE, more ASSAULT, more pressure on the Toll from the start. The Known Lands feel much smaller when the Shadowking is the only enemy.

## Winning the Game

### The Territory Victory

At the end of every round, after War Banners are generated, the game checks: does any Arch-Regent hold the Heartstone AND control more Strongholds than everyone else? If yes: Territory Victory. That player wins.

To hold the Heartstone, your Fellowship must occupy the Dark Fortress when no Death Knights are present — then spend 1 action to Reclaim it. The Heartstone token moves to your Court and stays visible to all players. If you hold it and lose a War Field combat, it drops to your current node and any Fellowship there can pick it up.

**Tiebreaker:** If the Heartstone-holder is tied for most Strongholds: (1) highest current War Banner count wins, (2) if still tied, disclosed coin flip. The tiebreaker result is shown before the win sequence.

### The Shadowking Victory

Doom Toll reaches 13. All players lose. In Blood Pact mode, the traitor wins alone.

### The Draw

All active Arch-Regents simultaneously enter Broken Court state. The game ends. No one wins. The Shadowking did not need to ring thirteen bells — he simply waited until you exhausted each other.

## Reading the Board at a Glance

The persistent UI always shows you, without opening any panel:

- The **Doom Toll position** and whether Final Phase is active
- Each player's **Stronghold count** (the territory victory stakes)
- Each player's **War Banner count** (the resource pressure)
- Each player's **Broken Court status** where applicable
- The current **round number**
- Whose **action phase** is active, or that a **Voting Phase** is in progress

The player in the lead is visible from the main board view. So is the player who needs rescue. So is the Doom Toll, always, in the corner of your eye, never far from the center of the screen.

## The First Five Turns: *Into the Blighted Wastes*

If this is your first session, the game opens with a mandatory guided tutorial — five turns that introduce one mechanic each, in the order the real game requires them.

| Turn | What You Do | What You Learn |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **1 — March from Your Keep** | Move your Fellowship to the first unclaimed Stronghold | Movement, War Banner costs, claiming |
| **2 — Send the Herald Ahead** | Deploy your Herald alone to the next Stronghold | Recruiting Unknown Wanderers, Diplomatic Protection |
| **3 — Your First Battle** | A rival Arch-Regent contests your Stronghold | War Field resolution, Fate Cards, Penalty Cards |
| **4 — The Toll Strikes** | The Shadowking draws his first Behavior Card | The Doom Toll, the Voting Phase, the cost of abstaining |
| **5 — Claim the Forge Keep** | Reach a Forge Keep Stronghold | The production loop, the strategic value of Forge Keeps |

After five turns the tutorial ends and the real game begins. In later sessions, the tutorial can be skipped from the settings menu. Contextual tooltips will surface when you encounter a mechanic for the first time — rescuing an ally, fighting a Death Knight, reaching Final Phase. These are always dismissable and never block your action.

## Quick Reference

**Actions per turn:** 2 (1 if Broken Court)

**War Banner cost:** 1 per node traversed · 1 to claim a Stronghold

**War Banner production:** 1 per Artificer per turn · 3 per Artificer at a Forge Keep

**Fellowship max size:** 8 characters (including Arch-Regent)

**Fate Card hand:** Starting hand 3 · Base limit 3 · +1 per additional Herald · Max 6 · Replenished each Production Phase · Combat draws are temporary (not from personal hand)

**Hand limit table:** 0–1 Heralds → 3 · 2 Heralds → 4 · 3 Heralds → 5 · 4+ Heralds → 6

**Behavior Deck composition:** 6 SPAWN · 6 MOVE · 4 CLAIM · 3 ASSAULT · 1 ESCALATE

**Doom Toll advances:** Non-unanimous vote · Deck reshuffle · Blight Wraith claims Forge Keep · Arch-Regent enters Broken Court

**Doom Toll recedes:** Defeat a Death Knight · Reclaim a Forge Keep from Wraiths · Unanimous vote by 3+ Arch-Regents who all spend cards · Herald Diplomatic Action (−2)

**Final Phase:** Doom Toll position 10+ · Two Behavior Cards per round · Wraiths auto-spread · Voting costs 2 Fate Cards · HUD shows ~rounds remaining

**Final Phase strategy (~2–4 rounds):** Consolidate, push for Heartstone, or contain the leader — there is no third option

**Broken Court:** Penalty Cards ≥ War Banner count · 1 action/turn · No initiating combat · No claiming · No recruiting · Full vote retained

**Recovery:** Active Arch-Regent spends 1 action, donates 2–5 Fate Cards · Rescued player restores Banners, clears Penalty Cards

**3-Player:** Doom Toll starts at position 2 · Unanimity requires all three players · One abstention is 33% defection · Broken Court player = 33% of coalition · Accusation requires both remaining players unanimously
