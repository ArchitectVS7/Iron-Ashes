# Design v2 — Focus Group, Round 3 (Injecting Passion & Complexity)

> Status: Stage-5 creative review — the reconvened panel on "the game is sound but emotionally thin."
> Date: 2026-06-22
> Method: The same five isolated-context panelists (board-game designer, video-game designer, D&D DM,
> MTG judge, Warhammer 40K referee), no cross-talk. Briefed on the current v2 build, the locked balance,
> the player-voice signal (an 8-persona v1 market study that named RESCUE the signature moment), and the
> "cutting-room-floor" v1 ideas (the Herald, Herald→hand-capacity, a vassal/binding layer). Each asked:
> (Q1) where's the missing passion + the single highest-leverage add; (Q2) keep/adapt/reject the cut
> ideas; (Q3) one new strategic axis worth the complexity. Companion: `DESIGN-V2-FOCUS-GROUP-R2.md`.

---

## 0. The one-line finding

**The missing passion is persistent, witnessed, *betrayable* relationship.** Today every interaction is a
one-shot transaction that evaporates — nothing *accrues* between two players, so the only entity you have
a running story with is the AI. Four of five panelists independently proposed the same spine: **make
debt between players a standing, public, breakable bond.** The fifth (judge) attacked the same flatness
from a different seam (the Pledge is a solved public scalar). All five agree: **do NOT take every idea —
that rebuilds v1's sprawl. Spend the complexity budget on one tight spine.**

## 1. Unanimous diagnosis

| Point | Voices |
|---|---|
| **Interaction, not "passion," is the problem** — relationships are one-shot and evaporate; coordination, not rivalry. "Parallel solitaire wearing a passion costume." | all 5 |
| **The only running *story* is with the dark** (the grudge-steer) — which is also the best feature; players have no equivalent *with each other*. | board, video, DM |
| **Rescue lands because it's the one *witnessed bilateral debt*** between two named players — it should be a renewable VERB, not a rare capped event. | board, video, DM |
| **Keep the social layer OFF the scarce card economy** — oaths must be cheap/free to swear; the cost is the *broken-promise risk*, not cards (else players can't afford to be social). | DM (sharp), board |
| **Don't pile new systems** — Herald + vassal + new axis = v1 sprawl reborn. Spend the budget on ONE. | judge, board |

## 2. The convergent spine — OATHS (+ the dark's memory)

Three panelists proposed, in their own words, the **same mechanic**: a **public, breakable, two-player
bond** that makes rescue's feeling renewable.

- **The Oath** (board "Oath" / video "Oath" / DM "Oaths"): during PLEDGE or via Rescue, a player may
  **swear an Oath** to another — "I'll cover your strike / I won't Raid you / I'll pledge for your node."
  It's a **public token between the two Keeps** with a visible **strain meter**. Cheap (or free) to swear;
  the weight is in the **break**.
- **Oathbreaking is the clip moment** (video): any sworn player may break it for a burst (refill cards /
  a free Raid / steal a banner) — but it's **loud** (token shatters, the betrayed gets a one-round
  **Vendetta**: a free Raid + the dark's steer auto-points at the breaker).
- **It ABSORBS Rescue** (all three): rescue becomes the *dramatic, earned* Oath (a rescued rival is
  auto-**Sworn**). One social spine, two intensities — NOT two overlapping systems.
- **Crucially — this dissolves the Stage-5d rescue cap.** The "witnessed bilateral debt" feeling now
  fires **every round** via Oaths instead of being gated on the ~1/game break/rescue rate. You stop
  needing more Breaks (which hit the all_broken wall); the Oath carries the social density, and Rescue
  stays the rare peak.

**The DM's other half — THE LEDGER (the villain's memory).** Make the grudge *persistent*: the dark keeps
a public **hit-list**; wounding it (DK kills, denying seams) climbs your name; it **hunts the top of the
Ledger** (leader as tiebreaker). And **breaking an Oath jumps you to the top** — the dark is the *enforcer
of betrayal*. This turns the villain from weather into a nemesis with memory and makes the grudge-steer a
shared political object ("don't put me top of the Ledger — I'll pledge for your Keep"). The Devil's
Bargain, finally load-bearing.

## 3. The cutting-room ideas — the verdicts

- **The Herald — KEEP, but ADAPT (near-unanimous).** Everyone wants the **non-card, positional verb
  against the dark** (precious, since everything else routes through Cards). **Fork on form:**
  - *As an ACTION* (board): a one-action **PARLEY** at a seam that cools the dark's escalation a step but
    **marks you as its next target** — no new piece, least fiddle.
  - *As a PIECE* (video, DM, 40K): a **lone vulnerable runner** — immune to rivals, **killable by the
    dark** — that walks to Parley; a multi-round gamble the table watches. 40K adds: it must pass rival
    **gates** (see below), so rivals *intercept by toll*, not attack.
- **Herald-count → hand-capacity — REJECT as written; ADAPT the GOAL (build identity) (4/5).** Raw
  "more Heralds = bigger hand" breaks the card-squeeze ("more cards is just better" — not a tradeoff).
  The clean version (judge + 40K): **Heralds are BODIES OFF THE BOARD** — fielding one (cap 0–2) raises
  your hand but removes a fighter ⇒ a real **hand-vs-board / political-vs-martial** axis (deep hand +
  anti-dark reach + *weak at holding territory*, vs fat board + fast claims + can't tap doom). DM/video
  prefer it as a **single up-front stance** (Court vs War). Either way: ONE legible identity choice.
- **Vassal/binding layer — REJECT standalone; ABSORB its soul (5/5).** A control-transfer vassal system
  is a **kingmaking / runaway-leader exploit** (the unanimity-vote pathology reborn — judge). Its soul is
  already delivered by **Oaths** (voluntary binding) + **Rescue** (earned binding). 40K's salvage:
  geography-derived **"Beholden"** — paying a rival's gate-toll binds you to them for the round (the map
  does the binding, no new verb).

## 4. Two orthogonal high-leverage levers (powerful — but phase them, don't pile)

- **Sealed core Pledge (the JUDGE's headline).** The Pledge is a *solved public scalar* (computable to
  the card — no bluff, no read, no regret). Make it **simultaneous + secret, revealed at once** → a
  **volunteer's-dilemma** (over-pledge = wasted offense; under-pledge = eat the strike; the leader-discount
  becomes a public target everyone reads). Manufactures interaction from a system that already exists,
  zero new scope. **Bonus:** it fixes the over-firing Gambit (28%) by *incentive* — a Gambit forces the
  dark to target you, sealed-pledged: "I bet my rivals won't bail me out." ⚠️ This **reverses the Round-1
  fork** (open live staredown, the video designer's championed beat) → a genuine lead-designer call.
- **Forge-as-Gate tolls (the 40K's headline).** Make the 4 Forges the **chokepoints** between the Keep
  belt and the Keystone: to march inward you pass *through* a Forge; a rival owner charges a **toll** (a
  Banner or Card, in the open). "I hold the gate, you owe me," realized literally, every turn — attrition
  geometry, organic alliances, a **positional tax on the front-runner** (catch-up valve), a Gambit run
  becomes a *visible siege*, and a DK on a Forge **closes the gate for everyone** (shared reason to
  Strike). Zero new currency. Its own balance project (guard toll-stacking/kingmaking — gates must be
  contestable, capped, forceable open).

## 5. Guards & worries (carry into any build)

- **Keep Oaths off the card economy** (DM) — or the social layer can't be afforded.
- **The 18–22% dark band will move** — the Ledger makes the dark *smarter* (raises it); the Herald's
  doom-relief *lowers* it. They balance against each other but MUST go through the sim, not vibes.
- **Exploit watch:** the **turtle-Herald doom-faucet lock** (judge — Herald relief must be exposed/
  consumable, never a safe faucet; watch turtle-win & dark-win as a *joint* band); **toll-stacking**
  (40K — gates contestable + capped); **oath-as-bookkeeping** (board — needs a collection *window* +
  public token, and **welching must be legal and ugly**, not impossible).
- **Shared action pool:** Oaths / Herald / gate-Strikes / Rescue all compete for ~2 actions — watch they
  don't starve each other (especially Rescue). Oaths should be cheap/free to swear for this reason.
- **Don't let the dark become wallpaper** while the table negotiates — the Ledger + the Herald-target
  keep it a character; protect those.

## 6. Synthesized recommendation

A coherent, scope-respecting path — a **spine** plus sequenced options:

1. **BUILD THE SPINE: Oaths + the Ledger.** The convergent 3/5 headline, it makes the villain a nemesis
   with memory, restores social density as a *renewable* verb, absorbs Rescue, and **elegantly dissolves
   the open 5d rescue-cap decision** (you no longer need 2–4 Breaks — the Oath carries the bilateral-debt
   feeling every round). Keep it off the card economy; give it a collection window + loud, legal welching.
2. **ADD IDENTITY: the political↔martial stance (Heralds-as-bodies, cap 0–2) + the Herald verb.** The
   near-unanimous "more than card-voting" answer — a build identity and a non-card lever against the dark.
   Decide the Herald's form (action vs lone-runner piece).
3. **THEN evaluate, one at a time (each its own balance pass):** **(a)** sealed core Pledge (also fixes
   the Gambit — but reverses the open-staredown choice), **(b)** Forge-gate tolls (positional density).
   Do NOT build all of §2–§4 at once — the panel is unanimous that that re-creates v1 sprawl.

The two genuine lead-designer forks to settle before building: **(i)** Herald as an *action* or a *piece*;
**(ii)** keep the *open* live Pledge or move to a *sealed* core Pledge (the Round-1 question, reopened).
