# v3 Teach Script — the canonical progressive-disclosure onboarding

> **Status: REQUIREMENT for the styled-UI pass (backlog T3-10), not a suggestion.** This document is the
> authoritative answer to review learnability findings #2/#3 (backlog T1-5): the game's natural act-staging
> IS the teaching order, so nothing below the fold is taught until the game itself makes it relevant. The
> styled UI must implement the upfront script as onboarding copy and each in-context item as a **one-shot
> beat that fires the first time its trigger occurs** (the T1-3 round-1 Crown callout and the T1-4 Last
> Stand prompt are the built prototypes of this pattern). A first-time table should hear the upfront
> section in **5–6 minutes** and nothing else before playing.
>
> Spec cross-reference: `DESIGN-V3-ALGORITHM.md` (engine truth). Where this script simplifies, the script
> wins *for teaching*; the spec wins for rules disputes.

## Part 1 — UPFRONT (5–6 minutes, before the first THREAT)

### 1.1 The four-idea spine (say these four things first)

1. **The dark is eating the map.** The Shadowking's Blight spreads every round toward the **Keystone** at
   the center. If it gets there, everyone loses. The villain is telegraphed — you always see what it's
   about to do.
2. **You are rivals forced to share a wall.** Each round the dark strikes, and the table **pledges cards
   together** to block it — then you spend your remaining turn fighting each other for land and retainers.
   Every card you spend on yourself is a card not holding the wall.
3. **You build a court, and courts can be broken.** Explore to meet named retainers; raid rivals to
   capture theirs. From **The March** (the middle act), losing everything means real **elimination**.
4. **Four ways to win, one shared way to lose.** Outlast everyone, hold the most land at the end, take the
   throne, or kill the dark itself. Let the Keystone fall, and nobody wins.

### 1.2 The round: THREAT → PLEDGE → your turn

- **THREAT:** the dark announces its strike — target and power, face up. No surprises from the villain.
- **PLEDGE:** everyone secretly commits cards against it; block it together or someone bleeds.
- **The Crown surcharge:** whoever leads holds the **Crown** — their pledged cards count for **less**, and
  the dark hunts them first. Leading is dangerous, on purpose. *(If the human starts as Crown, the round-1
  callout beat fires: "You hold the most land — the dark hunts YOU, and your pledged cards count for
  less.")*
- **ACTION:** then each player takes 2 actions. **DAWN** cleans up and the clock advances.

### 1.3 The two currencies

- **Cards** are your strength — pledged against the dark, spent in raids and stands. One pool, two wars.
- **Banners** are your income — generated by your land each Dawn, spent to equip and to pay ransoms.
- Rule of thumb to say out loud: *cards fight, banners fund.*

### 1.4 Your basic turn (the only verbs taught upfront)

- **MARCH** — move a piece.
- **CLAIM** — take a neutral Holding and **flip its face-down token**: you *meet* a named retainer who
  joins your court — or you hit a risk (the token's back-sigil hints which; it's press-your-luck, not
  blind, and a bad flip always leaves you a play).
- **RAID** — fight a rival where you stand. (What winning lets you *choose* is taught at your first win —
  see Part 2.)
- Everything else (oaths, ransom, the heart) introduces itself when it matters.

### 1.5 Elimination — the one warning that must land

Say exactly this: **"Nobody can die in the first act. From The March on, elimination is real — watch your
Exposure meter."** The meter (SAFE / can lose land / can be DEPOSED) is always visible, moves the moment
the act gate moves, and the game warns you one Dawn before deposal unlocks ("the tide has reached you").
If you are eliminated, you are **not** out of the game — but that's taught at the moment it happens
(Part 2), never upfront.

### 1.6 The end conditions — the taught version (exactly three sentences)

> **If the dark's Blight consumes the Keystone — or the last Warlord falls — the dark wins and everyone
> loses.** **Otherwise the winner is the last Warlord standing, or whoever holds the most land when the
> clock runs out.** **Two rare plays can steal it early: claim the throne with the Crown's Gambit, or kill
> the dark itself and win the scramble that follows.**

That is the whole teach. The engine's six-condition preempt ordering (`ALGORITHM §6`) is never spoken at
the table; the UI resolves edge cases and narrates the outcome.

## Part 2 — IN CONTEXT (one-shot beats, fired at first trigger)

Each item below is taught **only when its trigger first occurs**, as a short beat the player must
acknowledge (or a bark plus a persistent control). Triggers are per-game, once.

| # | Trigger (first time…) | The beat teaches |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | …the human **wins a RAID** | **The capture election:** pick exactly ONE — take the land, rout the piece, or (if you won big enough) **CAPTURE** a retainer to your Hold. Never land + capture together. A captured named retainer is a hostage, visible to all on the Hold rail. |
| C2 | …a piece of the human's is **captured** (or they hold their first captive) | **Ransom:** buy a captive back for cards + banners (part is destroyed — hostage-trading burns value). A freed piece comes home and can't be re-grabbed immediately. Freeing an **ally's** piece can forge an Oath. |
| C3 | …an **Oath** is first available (SWEAR_OATH legal, or an ally-ransom offer) | **Oaths:** a public sworn bond; breaking one has a price the whole table sees (the Ledger remembers). |
| C4 | …the **act turns to March** | The Exposure beat, escalated: "Elimination is now live — the meter is armed." (This re-teaches 1.5 at the moment it becomes true.) |
| C5 | …**Reckoning begins and the heart spawns** at the Keystone | **The dark can now be killed:** its heart sits exposed at the Keystone with a public HP track. **ASSAULT_HEART** commits a force over telegraphed rounds — those pieces can't also defend home. And: **the throne cannot be claimed while the heart beats there** (no accidental Gambit wins). |
| C6 | …a player is **eliminated** (the human, or the first at the table) | **The Death Bequest** (the exit beat: bequeath your captives/cards to a living player, or Death-Curse your killer) and **the Wraith**: the dead join the dark and get one bounded input per round — steer its wrath or feed its strike. Dead ≠ done; you are now someone the living must court. |
| C7 | …a rival is about to take the human's stronghold | **The Last Stand prompt** (human-facing since T1-4): the game STOPS and hands you the decision — commit how many cards? Projected totals are shown (ties go to the defender), with the warning that **committed cards are next round's Pledge cards**. Yielding on purpose is a legal, sometimes correct choice. |
| C8 | …the **Crown's Gambit** first becomes declarable by the human | **The Gambit:** a rare, telegraphed throne-grab — name the round, hold the Keystone, survive the table's response. (Blocked while the heart is exposed — C5.) |
| C9 | …the **dark's gaze** first pressures someone (the T2-2 Reckoning blight on the quietest seat — the SK bark "The quietest banner burns first" fires) | **Hiding is dangerous:** from Reckoning on, each Dawn the dark blights land of whoever has **engaged the dark least** (pledged/struck/assaulted least — Keeps are safe, and it never takes your last productive node). The blighted node ashes NEXT Dawn unless pushed back. Pledge, STRIKE, PARLEY, or hit the heart and the gaze moves to someone quieter; a live heart assault stops it for everyone. |

### Blood Pact — never in game one

**Blood Pact mode (the hidden traitor) is not taught, offered, or mentioned in a first game.** It is a
second-session mode for a table that already knows the base rhythm: the setup screen should present it as
an unlockable variant ("play your first game without it"), and its teach (the pact, the Audit, the
accusation gamble, the eliminated-traitor-still-wins twist) is a separate one-screen script shown only
when the mode is selected.
