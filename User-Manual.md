# Iron Throne of Ashes - Instruction Manual

*"Four ancient courts. One dying world. And a Shadowking who grows stronger while you squabble."*

Welcome to **Iron Throne of Ashes**, a dark fantasy strategy game of broken alliances, tenuous trust, and a looming threat that could consume you all. 

---

## Object of the Game

For a thousand years, peace was maintained by the Heartstone. Now, the Heartstone has been stolen by the Shadowking, and the realm is crumbling. You are the Arch-Regent of one of the Four Elemental Courts (Earth, Air, Fire, or Water). 

How you win depends on the mode you are playing:

- **Competitive Mode (Standard)**: Reclaim the Heartstone and hold the most territory (Strongholds) when the game ends, or be the last Arch-Regent left standing.
- **Cooperative Mode**: Form a temporary alliance to reclaim the Heartstone from the Dark Fortress *before* the Doom Toll reaches 13.
- **Blood Pact (Traitor Mode)**: Play as normal, but one player has secretly sworn fealty to the Shadowking! Their hidden goal is to ensure the Doom Toll completes its countdown without being discovered.

*Beware:* If the spectral bell known as the **Doom Toll** strikes thirteen times, the Shadowking's Blighted Wastes consume the Known Lands entirely, and everyone (except the Blood Pact traitor) loses!

---

## Components & Assets

### The Known Lands (The Board)
The board consists of 28 nodes connected by paths.
* **Standard Strongholds (22):** Basic territories your Court can claim.
* **Forge Keeps (4):** High-value corner strongholds. Controlling these grants massive resource production (+3 War Banners per turn instead of 1).
* **The Dark Fortress:** The Shadowking's unclaimable domain. Only a lone Herald may dare enter safely to negotiate.
* **Hall of Neutrality:** The neutral starting point of the stolen Heartstone.

### Your Fellowship (The Pieces)
Your Fellowship is your warband. Every character has a specific role and a Base Power level.
* **Arch-Regent (Power 8):** Your leader. Their presence dictates how many Fate Cards you draw in combat.
* **Knight (Power 6):** The muscle. Knights can claim strongholds, challenge the Shadowking's Death Knights, and fight rival Fellowships. They *cannot* recruit.
* **Herald (Power 0):** The diplomat. Only Heralds can reveal Unknown Wanderers scattered across the board. If a Herald travels alone, they gain *Diplomatic Protection*—rival players cannot attack them (though the Shadowking's forces don't care about diplomacy).
* **Artificer (Power 3):** The engine. Artificers generate your vital currency: War Banners. An Artificer at a Forge Keep produces 3 Banners per turn; anywhere else, they produce 1. Don't neglect them, or your war machine will halt!

### Resources & Cards
* **War Banners:** The currency of war. You spend them to move (1 per path), claim Strongholds (1 per claim), and boost your strength in combat. **Unspent Banners are discarded at the end of each round!**
* **Fate Cards:** Used during voting and combat. They have values ranging from -1 to 4. 
* **Penalty Cards:** Acquired from losing battles. If you accumulate more Penalty Cards than your current War Banners, your Court breaks.

### The Shadowking's Forces
The Shadowking has no player—he acts autonomously through a Behavior Deck!
* **Blight Wraiths (Power 6):** Shadowy forces that spread from the Dark Fortress, occupying Strongholds and disrupting your plans.
* **Death Knights (Power 10):** Powerful mobile lieutenants that actively hunt the strongest player. Defeating one reduces the Doom Toll.

---

## Setup
Each player begins at their Court's starting Forge Keep with a starting Fellowship consisting of their Arch-Regent, 1 Knight, 1 Herald, and 1 Artificer. 20 *Unknown Wanderer* tokens are placed face-down on Standard Strongholds.

---

## How to Play

A round consists of three main phases:

### Phase 1: The Shadowking Acts
The Shadowking takes his turn first by flipping a card from the **Behavior Deck**.
He might spawn new Wraiths, move his Death Knights toward the strongest player, or assault the weakest. 
*If the deck ever runs out and reshuffles, the Doom Toll advances!*

### Phase 2: The Voting Phase
Before the Shadowking's card takes effect, the table may vote to **COUNTER** it.
* **Unanimous Counter:** If *every* active player spends 1 Fate Card (2 in the Final Phase), the Shadowking's action is blocked!
* **Abstain:** If *even one* player chooses not to pay (or cannot afford to), the Shadowking's action happens, and **the Doom Toll advances by 1.**
*This is the core of the game! Do you spend your precious cards to help the leader, or do you let the world burn a little to save your strength?*

### Phase 3: Player Actions
Each player gets 2 actions per turn. You may:
* **Move:** Travel along connections by spending 1 War Banner per path.
* **Claim:** A Knight or Arch-Regent can spend 1 War Banner to claim an unoccupied Stronghold for your Court.
* **Recruit:** A Herald adjacent to an *Unknown Wanderer* token can spend an action to flip it. The revealed character (a Knight, Herald, or Artificer) instantly joins your Fellowship!
* **Diplomatic Mission:** A Herald traveling completely alone to the Dark Fortress can spend an action to negotiate. This permanently marks the Herald, but lowers the Doom Toll by 1!
* **Rescue:** If a rival is in *Broken Court* state, you can donate 2–5 Fate Cards to them. They immediately return to the game, and now they magically owe you a favor!

---

## Combat: The War Field

If two Fellowships end up on the same Stronghold, or you encounter the Shadowking's forces, **The War Field** is triggered!

1. **Calculate Base Strength:** Add together the Power Levels of all your present characters, plus your current number of War Banners.
2. **Draw Fate Cards:** 
   * The **Attacker** draws cards equal to their Arch-Regent's Power divided by 4 (rounded up). They pick one to play **face-down**.
   * The **Defender** draws one fewer card, and picks one to play **face-up**.
3. **Reveal & Resolve:** Add the chosen Fate Card value to your Base Strength. The highest total wins (ties go to the defender). 
4. **The Cost of Defeat:** The loser suffers a number of **Penalty Cards** equal to the difference in the final scores. 

*(Note: When fighting the Shadowking's forces, they do not draw Fate Cards. Death Knights have a flat strength of 10, Blight Wraiths have 6).*

## Advanced Rules

### Broken Court State
There is no player elimination! If you accumulate more Penalty Cards than your current War Banners, your Court is broken. 
* You are reduced to 1 action per turn.
* You cannot claim Strongholds, initiate combat, or recruit.
* **You still vote.** 

You must wait for another player to perform a **Rescue** action to bring you back into the fray. Will they do it out of the goodness of their heart? Unlikely. They want your vote. 

### The Final Phase
When the Doom Toll reaches **10**, the candles dim. The Final Phase begins. 
The Shadowking now draws *two* Behavior Cards per round. Blight Wraiths spread automatically every turn. Voting costs twice as many Fate Cards. The end is near. Resolve your differences quickly, or become ashes!

---

Good luck, Arch-Regent. Form alliances, betray them at the right moment, and whatever you do—don't let the 13th bell toll.
